# Handling Delicate Items

## What This Is

Delicate items (fragile plants, special flowers, sensitive products) require special handling to minimize damage. They are picked **directly into customer-specific carts** during Master Picking, then the **entire cart is transferred** through to shipping without re-picking at Supermarket.

**Special Workflow for Delicate Items:**
1. **Master Picking** - Pick into customer-specific carts (not mixed zone carts)
2. **Cart Transfer** - Same transfer process
3. **Supermarket Picking** - Grab entire cart (no item-by-item re-picking)

**Benefit:** Items are only physically handled ONCE, reducing damage risk.

## What Makes an Item "Delicate"

**Items are marked delicate in the system when:**
- Fragile plant varieties (delicate flowers, sensitive stems)
- High-value specialty items
- Items prone to damage from multiple handling
- VIP customer orders requiring extra care

**How to identify:**
- Item card has "Delicate" flag checked
- System automatically assigns to customer-specific carts
- Cart labels may show "DELICATE" or customer name

## How Delicate Items Flow Through the System

### Phase 1: Master Picking (Special Process)

**For Master Pickers:**

1. Open Master Pick Ticket (any run number)
2. System identifies delicate items automatically
3. **For delicate items:**
   - System assigns **customer-specific cart**
   - Cart is labeled with customer name (not just cart number)
   - You place delicate items directly into that customer's cart
   - Other items for that customer also go in same cart

4. **For regular items on same ticket:**
   - Go into standard mixed zone carts as usual

5. Record quantities as normal
6. Complete picking and quality checks
7. **Mark delicate carts with special handling labels**

**Important:** 
- You may have both regular mixed carts AND customer-specific delicate carts on the same ticket
- Keep them separate and clearly labeled
- Handle delicate item carts with extra care

### Phase 2: Cart Transfer

**For Transfer Staff:**

1. Delicate item carts transfer same as regular carts
2. **Extra care during movement:**
   - Don't stack items on top
   - Minimize bouncing/jarring
   - Keep upright if marked
   - Transfer promptly (don't let sit)

3. Post transfer shipment and receipt as normal
4. Alert Supermarket team about delicate carts

### Phase 3: Supermarket Picking (Simplified)

**For Supermarket Pickers:**

1. Open Supermarket Pick Ticket
2. System shows which carts contain items
3. **For carts marked as delicate or customer-specific:**
   - **Do NOT pick item-by-item**
   - **Grab the ENTIRE CART**
   - Verify cart contents match ticket
   - Record items in system
   - Complete quality checks ON THE CART
   - Stage cart for shipping

4. **For regular items from mixed carts:**
   - Pick item-by-item as normal into customer cart

**Important:** Delicate item carts skip the re-picking step entirely.

## Master Picker Responsibilities

### Setting Up Customer-Specific Carts

1. When you see delicate items on your ticket:
   - Get empty cart
   - Label with customer name clearly
   - Note "DELICATE" if applicable
   - System tracks cart assignment

2. Pick delicate items carefully:
   - Handle gently
   - Don't overfill cart
   - Keep items organized
   - Separate fragile from non-fragile within cart

3. Quality check thoroughly:
   - Delicate items need extra inspection
   - Check for damage before placing in cart
   - Don't pick damaged items
   - Report quality issues immediately

### Tips for Master Picking Delicate Items

✓ **Handle gently** - One touch principle  
✓ **Label carts clearly** - Customer name visible  
✓ **Don't overfill** - Give items space  
✓ **Organize thoughtfully** - Consider how customer will unpack  
✓ **Extra quality checks** - Inspect closely  
✓ **Report damage immediately** - Don't hide problems  
✓ **Keep cart upright** - Don't tip or tilt  
✓ **Transfer promptly** - Minimize time in cart

## Supermarket Picker Responsibilities

### Identifying Delicate Item Carts

**You'll know it's a delicate cart when:**

✓ Cart is labeled with customer name (not just number)  
✓ "DELICATE" marking visible  
✓ Ticket shows "customer-specific cart"  
✓ System note indicates special handling  
✓ Only one customer's items in the cart

### Handling Delicate Carts

1. **Locate the cart** - May be in special staging area
2. **Verify contents** without re-picking:
   - Visual count matches ticket
   - Customer name matches order
   - No obvious damage occurred during transfer

3. **Quality checks:**
   - Labels still correct
   - Quality still acceptable (check for transit damage)
   - Quantity appears correct
   - Water if needed (plants)

4. **Record in system:**
   - Mark items as "picked" even though you didn't physically re-pick
   - System needs to track completion
   - Note any issues found

5. **Stage for shipping:**
   - Keep cart intact
   - Place in appropriate shipping area
   - Mark "DELICATE" for loading team
   - Keep separate from regular items

## Common Situations

### "Should I re-pick these delicate items into a new cart?"

**NO - Leave them in the customer-specific cart!**
- The whole point is to avoid multiple handling
- Cart is already organized for customer
- Additional handling increases damage risk
- Only re-pick if cart is damaged or badly organized

### Items look damaged after transfer

**What to do:**
1. Don't touch them further
2. Take photos of damage
3. Report to supervisor immediately
4. Supervisor will contact customer
5. May need replacement items

### Customer has both delicate and regular items

**What to do:**
1. Delicate items stay in their customer-specific cart from master
2. Regular items you pick from mixed carts into another cart
3. Customer ends up with 2+ carts (delicate cart + regular cart(s))
4. Keep carts together for shipping
5. Label clearly

### Delicate cart has items for multiple customers

**This should NOT happen!**
- Delicate item carts are customer-specific by design
- Report to supervisor
- May need to re-pick to separate
- Master picker may have made error

## Where to Find This

**For Master Pickers:** Watch for delicate item flags during picking  
**For Supermarket Pickers:** Look for customer-labeled carts  
**Item Setup:** Item Card → Delicate checkbox  
**Used by:** Master Pickers, Supermarket Pickers, Transfer Staff  
**Configuration:** Setup by inventory/product team

## Benefits of This System

**For Product Quality:**
- Only handled once = less damage
- Items stay organized
- Less breakage and waste
- Better customer satisfaction

**For Efficiency:**
- Supermarket picking faster (grab whole cart vs. item-by-item)
- Less labor per order
- Fewer quality issues to resolve
- Simpler tracking

**For Customer:**
- Items arrive in better condition
- Professional appearance
- Consistent quality
- Reflects well on company

## Tips for Success

✓ **Recognize delicate items** - Check item flags  
✓ **Handle with extra care** - One touch when possible  
✓ **Label clearly** - Customer name + DELICATE  
✓ **Quality check thoroughly** - Catch issues early  
✓ **Communicate** - Let next phase know about delicate carts  
✓ **Don't re-pick** - Trust the system design  
✓ **Report damage** - Don't hide problems  
✓ **Stage properly** - Keep away from heavy traffic areas

## What Happens Next

After delicate item handling:
1. Delicate carts staged separately for loading
2. Loading team handles with extra care
3. May be loaded last (on top) to avoid crushing
4. Delivery driver alerted to delicate items
5. Customer receives items in good condition

**Your careful handling protects the product through the entire journey!**
