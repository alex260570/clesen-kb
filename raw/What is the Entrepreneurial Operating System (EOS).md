What is EOS?

EOS (the Entrepreneurial Operating System <sup>®</sup>) is a complete, proven operating system for entrepreneurial businesses that helps leadership teams clarify their vision, gain traction on it, and become a healthy, cohesive team. It's a set of simple, practical tools — not a theory. Hundreds of thousands of companies worldwide run on EOS.

What does EOS stand for?

EOS stands for Entrepreneurial Operating System. It is a proper noun — a specific system created by Gino Wickman, not a generic term.

Who created EOS?

Gino Wickman created EOS in the early 2000s and introduced it to the public in his 2007 book Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business. Gino co-founded EOS Worldwide with Don Tinney in 2008 to scale the system through a network of Professional EOS Implementers.

What are the Six Key Components of EOS?

Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, and Traction. These are the six areas every company must strengthen, and EOS provides the tools to strengthen each one. Each Component has a dedicated deep-dive book in the EOS Mastery Series.

What size company is EOS right for?

EOS is designed for privately held entrepreneurial companies with roughly 10 to 250 employees. More importantly, EOS is for leadership teams who are open, honest, growth-oriented, and willing to be vulnerable.

How long does it take to implement EOS?

The full EOS Process takes approximately two years with a Professional EOS Implementer — typically 10 full days of sessions including a Focus Day, two Vision Building days, and ongoing Quarterly and Annual sessions. Companies using EOS Academy to self-implement move at their own pace.

Can we implement EOS ourselves?

Yes! Many companies successfully self-implement using EOS books, tools, and resources from EOS Academy. Working with an EOS Implementer can help you move faster and stay pure to the system.

What's the difference between EOS and other business systems?

EOS is a complete operating system covering all six areas of a business: Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, and Traction. Most other frameworks address only one area. EOS is designed to be implemented as a whole system — not mixed with other frameworks. See how EOS compares:
