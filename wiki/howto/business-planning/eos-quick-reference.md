## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is EOS?

**EOS** (Entrepreneurial Operating System®) is a complete, proven operating system for entrepreneurial businesses. It's a set of simple, practical tools—not a theory. Hundreds of thousands of companies worldwide run on EOS.

### What does EOS stand for?

EOS stands for **Entrepreneurial Operating System**. It is a proper noun—a specific system created by Gino Wickman, not a generic term.

### Who created EOS?

**Gino Wickman** created EOS in the early 2000s and introduced it to the public in his 2007 book *Traction: Get a Grip on Your Business*. Gino co-founded **EOS Worldwide** with Don Tinney in 2008 to scale the system through a network of Professional EOS Implementers.

### What are the Six Key Components of EOS?

1. **Vision** — Clarity on where you're going
2. **People** — Right people in right seats
3. **Data** — Running on objective measurables
4. **Issues** — Solving problems at the root
5. **Process** — Documenting and systemizing
6. **Traction** — Disciplined execution

Each component has a dedicated deep-dive book in the EOS Mastery Series.

### What size company is EOS right for?

EOS is designed for privately held entrepreneurial companies with roughly **10 to 250 employees**. More importantly, EOS is for leadership teams who are:

- Open and honest
- Growth-oriented
- Willing to be vulnerable
- Committed to discipline

### How long does it take to implement EOS?

**With a Professional EOS Implementer:** Approximately **18–24 months**
- Typically 10 full days of sessions
- Includes: 1 Focus Day + 2 Vision Building days + ongoing Quarterly & Annual sessions

**Self-Implementing:** **12–36 months** (your pace)
- Using EOS books, tools, and EOS Academy resources

### Can we implement EOS ourselves?

**Yes!** Many companies successfully self-implement using:

- *Traction* book by Gino Wickman
- Free tools at **eosworldwide.com/tools**
- **EOS Academy** self-serve courses
- Community support

Working with a Professional EOS Implementer can help you move faster and stay pure to the system.

### What's the difference between EOS and other business systems?

**EOS is a complete operating system** covering all six areas of business: Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, and Traction.

**Most other frameworks** address only one area (goals, agile, lean, etc.).

**EOS is designed** to be implemented as a whole system—not mixed with other frameworks.

### How does EOS compare to other frameworks?

EOS provides alternative approaches to:

- **Scaling Up** (Rockefeller Habits)
- **OKRs** (Objectives and Key Results)
- **4DX** (Four Disciplines of Execution)
- **Holacracy**
- **Agile / Scrum**
- **Lean / Six Sigma**
- **V2MOM** (Vision, Values, Methods, Obstacles, Measures)
- **Great Game of Business**

For detailed comparisons, visit **eosworldwide.com** and search "EOS vs [Framework]"

## The EOS Toolbox (Free Tools)

### Five Foundational Tools

**All available free at:** eosworldwide.com/tools

#### 1. Vision/Traction Organizer (V/TO)®

**Purpose:** Get everyone rowing in the same direction

**What it does:**
- Simplifies strategic planning
- Distills vision into simple, understandable points
- Maps where you're going and how you'll get there
- Gets vision out of your head and onto paper

**Uses:** Eight guiding questions to create alignment

**Key sections:**
- Core values
- 10-year vision
- 3-year picture
- 1-year goals
- Quarterly Rocks (3–7 priorities)

#### 2. Accountability Chart

**Purpose:** Clarify who's accountable for what

**What it does:**
- Shows organizational structure and roles
- Makes accountability crystal clear
- Identifies right people in right seats

#### 3. Scorecard

**Purpose:** Track the weekly numbers that matter

**What it does:**
- Dashboard of 5–10 key metrics
- Shows you the pulse of your business
- Enables early problem detection
- Creates data-driven decision making

#### 4. Rocks

**Purpose:** Set your top 3–7 priorities for 90 days

**What it does:**
- Focuses team on critical priorities
- Breaks annual goals into quarterly chunks
- Creates visible accountability
- Tracks progress weekly

#### 5. Level 10 Meeting

**Purpose:** Solve the most important issues weekly

**What it does:**
- Structured 90-minute weekly meeting agenda
- Builds team health and communication
- Creates disciplined accountability
- Generates momentum through consistent execution

**Agenda (90 minutes):**
1. Segue (5 min)
2. Scorecard (5 min)
3. Rock Status (5 min)
4. Headlines (5 min)
5. To-Do Review (5 min)
6. IDS (50 min)
7. Cascade (5 min)

### Additional Tools

- **People Analyzer** — Assess people fit to roles
- **IDS** — Identify, Discuss, Solve process for problem-solving
- **Process Documenter** — Document core business processes
