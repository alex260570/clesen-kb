# EOS Six Key Components Guide

Comprehensive guide to each of the Six Key Components that every business must strengthen to achieve peak performance.

---

## Overview

The EOS Model teaches that **every business must get six things right**. Strengthening these components from weak (0%) to strong (80%+) moves a company into the top 5% of performers in any industry.

**Assessment:** Take the free [Organizational Checkup](https://organizationalcheckup.com/) to score your company across all six. Most companies start at 20–40%.

---

## Component 1: Vision

### What It Means

Getting **100% of your leadership team on the same page** about where you're going and how you'll get there.

### Why It Matters

- Prevents endless re-discussion of direction
- Aligns resources to priorities
- Enables clear decision-making
- Attracts aligned team members
- Creates motivation and purpose

### Key Elements

| Element | Definition | Timeframe |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Core Values** | Who you are and what you stand for | Timeless |
| **10-Year Vision** | The ultimate destination | 10 years out |
| **3-Year Picture** | What success looks like | 3 years out |
| **1-Year Goals** | What you'll achieve this year | 12 months |
| **90-Day Rocks** | Top 3–7 priorities | Quarter |
| **Quarterly Plan** | Execution roadmap | 3 months |

### Primary Tool

**Vision/Traction Organizer (V/TO)®** — One-page document capturing all vision elements.
- [[eos-vision-traction-organizer]]

### Measurement

- How clear is your vision across the leadership team?
- Can every leader articulate the same 10-year direction?
- Are annual goals broken into quarterly priorities?
- Do decisions align with stated core values?

## Component 2: People

### What It Means

**Right People in the Right Seats** — Ensuring every role is filled by someone with the capability, capacity, and cultural fit to excel.

### Why It Matters

- Eliminates "managing out" of poor performers
- Increases accountability and ownership
- Reduces constant supervision
- Improves morale (right team = great culture)
- Accelerates execution
- Reduces turnover

### Key Elements

| Element | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Right People** | Core values aligned; coachable; willing to grow |
| **Right Seats** | Role matches person's GWC (Get it, Want it, Capacity for it) |
| **Accountability** | Clear roles, expectations, metrics |
| **Performance** | Consistent execution and results |
| **Development** | Coaching to close capability gaps |

### Primary Tools

**Accountability Chart** — Org chart showing: who does what, how they're accountable
- [[eos-accountability-chart]]

**People Analyzer** — Assessment tool for evaluating fit
- [[eos-people-analyzer]]

### Measurement

- Does every leader fill the Right Seat?
- Are underperformers being managed?
- Is core values alignment enforced?
- Do people understand their accountabilities?
- Is your bench strength visible?

### Key Principle: GWC

**G** = **Get it** — Do they understand the role?
**W** = **Want it** — Do they want to do the work?
**C** = **Capacity for it** — Do they have the skill, time, energy?

If any is "no," they're in the wrong seat.

## Component 3: Data

### What It Means

**Running your business on objective measurables** — Using key metrics and KPIs to see the truth about your business, not feelings or opinions.

### Why It Matters

- Removes opinion and emotion from decisions
- Surfaces problems early
- Enables faster problem-solving
- Creates accountability
- Provides early warning signals
- Eliminates "surprises"

### Key Elements

| Element | Definition | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Scorecard** | 5–10 key metrics per department | Weekly |
| **On Track** | Status of priorities (Green/Yellow/Red) | Weekly |
| **Key Metrics** | Revenue, profit, employee count, pipeline | Weekly/Monthly |
| **Leading Indicators** | Metrics that predict future performance | Weekly |
| **Lagging Indicators** | Metrics that show past performance | Weekly |

### Primary Tool

**Scorecard** — Dashboard of 5–10 key numbers tracked weekly
- [[eos-scorecard]]

### Measurement

- Do you have 5–10 key metrics per department?
- Are they tracked weekly?
- Does everyone know what numbers drive the business?
- Do people course-correct based on metrics?
- Are metrics objective, not subjective?

### Key Principle: Keep It Simple

- **5–10 metrics total** — Not 50. The vital few.
- **Weekly cadence** — Not monthly or quarterly. Early detection.
- **Visible to all** — Everyone sees the numbers; everyone owns them.
- **Actionable** — Metrics should drive daily/weekly decisions.

## Component 4: Issues

### What It Means

**Identifying, discussing, and solving issues at the root** — So the same problems don't resurface again and again.

### Why It Matters

- Prevents recurring problems
- Builds trust (issues can be addressed)
- Improves team health
- Increases execution speed
- Creates learning culture
- Strengthens leadership team

### The IDS® Process

EOS teaches a structured problem-solving method called **IDS** (Identify, Discuss, Solve):

1. **Identify** — Surface the real problem (not a symptom)
2. **Discuss** — Have the conversation; explore root cause
3. **Solve** — Create and commit to a solution

### Primary Tool

**IDS Issue Solving Track** — Structured template for problem-solving
- [[eos-ids-issue-solving]]

### Types of Issues

| Type | Example | Owner |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Organizational** | Process not being followed | Leadership |
| **People** | Role fit or performance | Department head |
| **Execution** | Rock not on track | Accountable person |
| **External** | Market shift; competitor | Leadership team |

### Measurement

- Are issues being surfaced or hidden?
- Are issues solved at root or repeatedly?
- Do people trust they can raise issues?
- How many recurring issues exist?
- How fast are issues resolved?

### Key Principle: Create Safety

- Issues must be safe to raise without punishment
- Root causes must be sought, not blamed
- Solutions must be owned and followed up

## Component 5: Process

### What It Means

**Documenting and systematizing core processes** — So work gets done consistently, quality improves, and the business becomes scalable.

### Why It Matters

- Creates consistency
- Enables faster onboarding
- Reduces quality variation
- Allows delegation
- Builds scalable systems
- Prevents knowledge loss when people leave
- Removes dependency on individuals

### Key Elements

| Element | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Core Processes** | 5–10 processes that define how you operate |
| **Documentation** | Step-by-step, simple, visual |
| **Compliance** | Everyone follows documented process |
| **Continuous Improvement** | Regular review and updates |
| **Ownership** | Each process has an owner |

### Primary Tool

**Process Documenter** — Template for documenting core processes
- [[eos-process-documentation]]

### Core Processes to Document

Typically 5–10 processes that define your business:

| Example: Service Company | Example: Product Company |
|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Lead intake and qualification | Product development |
| Proposal and close | Manufacturing |
| Onboarding | Customer acquisition |
| Service delivery | Order fulfillment |
| Customer retention | Customer support |

### Measurement

- Are your core 5–10 processes documented?
- Are people following documented processes?
- How often are processes reviewed?
- Is process improvement happening?
- Are new hires trained on processes?

### Key Principle: Start Simple

- **Not every process** — Just the core 5–10 that define you
- **Simple documentation** — Flow charts, checklists, not 100-page manuals
- **Visual** — Pictures and flow diagrams beat text
- **Actionable** — Must be usable by someone new to the role

## Component 6: Traction

### What It Means

**Executing your vision with discipline and accountability** — Taking grand vision and breaking it into executable quarterly priorities (Rocks) that get done.

### Why It Matters

- Turns strategy into action
- Creates accountability
- Ensures vision progress
- Builds discipline
- Creates momentum
- Proves the system works

### Key Elements

| Element | Definition | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Rocks** | Top 3–7 priorities for quarter | Quarterly |
| **Level 10 Meeting** | Weekly structured team meeting | Weekly |
| **On Track Status** | Green/Yellow/Red on priorities | Weekly |
| **Scorecard** | Metrics tracked | Weekly |
| **To-Do's** | Weekly commitments | Weekly |
| **IDS** | Issues solved | Weekly |

### Primary Tools

**Level 10 Meeting®** — Structured weekly 90-minute meeting agenda
- [[eos-level-10-meeting]]

**Rocks** — 90-day priorities and goals
- [[eos-rocks]]

### The Weekly Rhythm

**Level 10 Meeting (90 min):**

1. **Segue** (5 min) — Personal/professional updates
2. **Scorecard** (5 min) — KPI review
3. **Rock Status** (5 min) — Priority status (Green/Yellow/Red)
4. **Customer/Employee Headlines** (5 min) — Key feedback
5. **To-Do Review** (5 min) — Prior week commitments
6. **IDS** (50 min) — Identify and solve top issues
7. **Cascade** (5 min) — Conclude and cascade

### The Quarterly Rhythm

**Quarterly Session (4 hours):**

1. **Review** — Assess prior quarter results
2. **Rock Setting** — Define 3–7 priorities for next quarter
3. **Rocks and To-Dos** — Cascade down organization
4. **Conclude** — Communicate progress

### Measurement

- Are Rocks set quarterly and tracked weekly?
- Are Level 10 Meetings happening on schedule?
- What % of Rocks achieved last quarter?
- How fast are issues resolved in meetings?
- Is discipline consistent?

### Key Principle: Quarterly Rhythm

- **Rocks set quarterly** — Not ad-hoc priorities
- **Tracked weekly** — Visual progress toward goal
- **Course corrected in real-time** — Don't wait until quarter-end
- **All organizational levels** — Rocks cascade down
- **Accountability** — Each Rock has an owner

## Component Strength Assessment

### Scoring

EOS assesses strength on a **0–100% scale** per component:

| Score | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **0–20%** | Not started or very weak | Major gap; immediate attention |
| **20–40%** | Basic foundation; inconsistent | Build foundation |
| **40–60%** | Moderate; some execution | Strengthen and systematize |
| **60–80%** | Good; mostly consistent | Tighten and improve |
| **80–100%** | Strong; consistently executed | Maintain and refine |

### Target

Goal: **Get all six components to 80%+**

When all six reach 80%+ strength:
- Business operates in top 5% of performers
- Growth is intentional and scalable
- Profit is predictable
- Team is healthy and aligned

### Path to 80%

1. **Assess current state** (Organizational Checkup)
2. **Prioritize weakest components**
3. **Strengthen one component at a time**
4. **Use corresponding tools**
5. **Build discipline around tools**
6. **Quarterly review and improvement**
7. **Celebrate progress**

## The Interconnection

These six components do not work in isolation. **They work together as a system:**

```
Strong Vision sets direction
         ↓
Right People execute it
         ↓
Data shows progress
         ↓
Issues get solved
         ↓
Processes create consistency
         ↓
Traction disciplines execution
         ↓
Back to Data (and cycle repeats)
```

When one component is weak, others suffer:
- **Weak Vision** → No clarity; people pull in different directions
- **Weak People** → Even good vision fails in execution
- **Weak Data** → Problems hide; decisions are guesses
- **Weak Issues** → Same problems recur; morale drops
- **Weak Process** → Inconsistency; scaling is impossible
- **Weak Traction** → Vision stays a dream; no progress
