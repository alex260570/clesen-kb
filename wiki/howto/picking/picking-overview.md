# Picking Processes Overview

Welcome to the Picking Guide! This guide explains all the picking processes you'll use in your daily work.

## Quick Start

**New to picking?** Start here:
1. Read [[master-picking-process]] - This is what you'll do most days
2. Practice with your supervisor
3. Refer back to this guide when you need help

## Daily Picking Workflows

### Main Picking Methods

| Process | When to Use | How Often | Document |
|---------|------------|-----------|----------|
| **Master Picking** | Regular daily orders, multiple customers | Every day | [[master-picking-process]] |
| **Supermarket Picking** | Single customer orders, rush orders | As needed | [[supermarket-picking-process]] |
| **Direct Location Pickup** | Customer pickups, will-call orders | As needed | [[direct-location-pickup]] |
| **Escalation Orders** | Urgent problem orders that need special attention | As needed | [[picking-escalations]] |

## I Need To...

### Starting Your Day

**"I just arrived. What do I do first?"**
- Check for [[master-picking-process#step-1-find-your-picking-ticket|Master Pick Tickets]] assigned to you
- Look for any urgent [[picking-escalations|Escalation Orders]]
- Start with Master Picking if tickets are ready

**"I don't see any tickets assigned to me"**
- Ask your supervisor - tickets generate automatically overnight
- Check if orders were locked (should happen automatically)
- There may not be orders shipping today

### During Picking

**"How do I start picking my ticket?"**
- See [[master-picking-process#step-2-start-picking|Master Picking Step 2: Start Picking]]
- Or [[supermarket-picking-process#step-3-start-picking|Supermarket Picking Step 2: Start Picking]]

**"I can't find an item in the bin"**
- Check nearby bins first
- Verify you're in the correct location
- Report to supervisor immediately
- See [[master-picking-process#common-issues|Common Issues]]

**"There's not enough of this item"**
- Pick what you can
- Record the shortage (Quantity Cut)
- Select a reason code
- Tell supervisor right away

**"An item looks damaged"**
- Don't pick damaged items
- Note it on your ticket
- Report to supervisor
- They'll find a replacement

**"I'm getting an error when I try to start"**
- Common: [[master-picking-process#not-all-items-are-available-for-picking|Not all items available]]
- Ask supervisor - they may need to check inventory
- May need to create an escalation order

### Finishing Up

**"How do I finish my picking ticket?"**
- Click **Finish Picking** button
- Complete all [[master-picking-process#step-6-quality-checks|Quality Checks]]
- Make sure all four boxes are checked

**"What do I do with the picked items?"**
- Leave carts in designated staging area
- Items will be loaded onto trucks
- Next team handles shipping

**"I have questions about my performance"**
- Your supervisor can show you:
  - How many units you picked
  - How long it took
  - Accuracy rate
- Ask for feedback anytime

## Understanding Ticket Types

### Master Pick Tickets
- **Most common** - You'll do these daily
- Pick for multiple customers at once
- Items organized by location to save walking
- Use numbered carts
- [[master-picking-process|Full Guide]]

### Supermarket Pick Tickets
- **Single customer focus**
- Rush or special orders
- More flexible than Master Picking
- Often urgent
- [[supermarket-picking-process|Full Guide]]

### Direct Location Tickets
- **Pickup orders**
- Items from one location
- Customer may be waiting
- Simpler process
- [[direct-location-pickup|Full Guide]]

### Escalation Tickets
- **Problem orders**
- High priority
- Need special attention
- Drop other work to handle these
- [[picking-escalations|Full Guide]]

## What Happens Automatically

### Every Night (Around 2 AM)
- System locks tomorrow's orders
- Creates Master Pick tickets
- Assigns items to carts
- Checks inventory levels

**You'll see:** Fresh tickets ready when you arrive in the morning.

### During the Day
- Urgent orders create Supermarket tickets
- Escalations are processed for problem orders
- System updates as you pick

- **Your Supervisor:** First stop for any questions
- **Experienced Pickers:** Great for practical tips
- **Warehouse Manager:** For process questions

### Common Questions
- "Which ticket do I do first?" → Ask supervisor, but usually Master Picks
- "Can I take a break during picking?" → Ask supervisor, depends on urgency
- "What if I make a mistake?" → Tell supervisor immediately, don't hide it
- "Can I pick faster?" → Ask experienced pickers for tips, but accuracy first

## Quality and Accuracy

### The Four Checks
Every ticket must have all four checks completed:
1. **Labels Checked** ✓ - Correct labels on items
2. **Quality Checked** ✓ - Items in good condition
3. **Quantity Checked** ✓ - Counts are accurate
4. **Water Checked** ✓ - Plants watered if needed

### Why This Matters
- Customers depend on getting exactly what they ordered
- Wrong items cost time and money to fix
- Quality problems hurt our reputation
- Accurate counts prevent inventory issues

## Safety and Best Practices

✓ **Lift properly** - Bend knees, not back  
✓ **Watch for wet floors** - Especially in plant areas  
✓ **Use carts correctly** - Don't overload  
✓ **Report spills** - Right away, even small ones  
✓ **Wear proper shoes** - No open-toed shoes in warehouse  
✓ **Stay hydrated** - Keep water with you  
✓ **Take breaks** - Don't push yourself too hard

## Tips from Experienced Pickers

💡 **"Pick in the order shown"** - System organizes by location to save steps  
💡 **"Count twice, pick once"** - Accuracy saves time in the long run  
💡 **"Report problems early"** - Don't wait until end of day  
💡 **"Keep your carts organized"** - Easier to load later  
💡 **"Learn item locations"** - You'll get faster over time  
💡 **"Ask questions"** - Better than guessing wrong

## Performance and Goals

### What We Measure
- **Units per hour** - How fast you pick
- **Accuracy rate** - How often you pick correct quantities
- **Quality issues** - Damaged items, wrong items
- **On-time completion** - Finishing by target time

### How to Improve
- Stay organized
- Learn item locations
- Work steadily (speed comes with practice)
- Ask for feedback regularly
- Watch how experienced pickers work
